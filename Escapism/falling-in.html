Falling In

Piece Information

  • Dimensions (in): 25 x 19.5
  • Materials:
    • Charcoal
    • Colored Pencil
    • Print Ink
    • Canson Paper
  • Estimated Pricing: 100$
  • Month Produced: 05/2025

After a year of exploring escapism, Ronit Nemenman determined that she should start shifting back into reality, and she hopes to stay there as long as possible. However, the reasons she initially sought escape have not changed, so she believes she will eventually fall back into her escapism. The realistic rendering of portraiture that blends into a smooth and idealistic texture for the hair reflects her gradual warping of reality, juxtaposed with the almost cartoonish stamps that represent her ways of escaping. These stamps become brighter in color as her hopes to escape rise to the surface, creating a narrative of returning to escapism. The repetitive motif of contrasting black-and-white versus colored scenes continues throughout.
