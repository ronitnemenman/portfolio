Golden Box

Piece Information

  • Dimensions (in):22.5 x 18
  • Materials:
    • Charcoal
    • KingArt Water Soluble Crayons
    • Prisma Color Pencil
  • Month Produced: 10/2024

Golden Box was the visual representation of Ronit Nemenman's feelings after her grandfather's death. She pushed herself into escapism until reaching a breaking point, when she couldn't stop remembering. Just as the poem itself is in black and white, the box is almost entirely inconspicuous, yet its gold coloring draws the viewer's eye, signaling its importance despite its plain shape and style.

The following is a collection of process and progress photos taken during the creation of this piece along with explanations

A still life set up with personal cues made from the poem that inspire the piece as shown with a few excerpts on the right

"A golden box and stories
Are all I have left
...
Soaked tissues in my pocket
A pocket knife you cherished
...
The stories kept me alive
I only felt smiles that barely held
Only felt tears not for me
...
I cried for you
I cried because I haven't let myself
I cried because I realized I was alone
I cried in the dark with a closed door
Protected from the world
I cried with music blasting
...
I cried for the stories I heard
In front of a golden box
And a wreath of flowers
...
That golden box into the ground
The one with your name on it
And you inside it"

A timelapse of Ronit Nemenman beginning her work in charcoal, showing the many small adjustments she made to the spacing, the piece, and the still life itself.

After working in charcoal for about a week, the piece was ready to seal and cover in water soluble crayon to create a subtle but unmistakable gold.

Using a ruler, Ronit Nemenman wrote as much of the poem as she could to create a blurred yet meaningful background for the piece.
