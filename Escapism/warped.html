Warped

Piece Information

  • Dimensions (in): 31 x 16.5
  • Materials:
    • Wood
    • Caran d'Ache Water Soluble Crayon
    • Dotting Paint Marker
    • Drawing Gum
    • Water Soluble Color Pencil
    • Various Other Water Soluble Crayons
  • Estimated Pricing: 125$
  • Month Produced: 10/2024

This piece represents the most literal definition of escapism. It depicts Ronit Nemenman observing reality but choosing to ignore it in favor of something more preferable.

The following is a collection of process and progress photos taken during the creation of this piece along with explanations

Initially, Ronit tested four different methods to keep the tentacles clear of the dark green on the wood so that the tentacles would appear to truly glow.

A process photo shows Ronit peeling off the resists during the next step of testing.

The tests ultimately determined that masking fluid was the best option. It prevented paint from leaking through the wood grain, allowed precise control of the shapes that tape could not provide, and avoided fracturing the wood like glues would.

A progress photo shows the drawing gum in place on the wood before Ronit began using water-soluble crayons.

Ronit then cut out the scene from the wood with a jigsaw, selecting the shape of the substrate.

Process photo of the peeling of the drawing gum to reveal the would before layering.

Close up of the various textures such as the ice, the grass, the tentacles, and their glow.
