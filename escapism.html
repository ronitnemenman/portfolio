Exploring Escapism

How does Ronit warp the world around her to escape reality? Utilizing objects and portraiture, she builds a narrative about ignoring and/or changing the world when seeking escape. What causes her to attempt to hide from reality? Through portraiture, Ronit reveals the reasons behind her desire to escape, while symbolic objects represent the causes themselves. How can she become more attentive to the world around her? By creating abstract pieces that encourage presence in the moment, drawing from observation and striving for realistic portraiture, she confronts the world head-on. Will Ronit be able to remain anchored in reality as time goes on?

Learning new techniques and experimenting with various materials, Ronit discovered the best way to both hide from and ultimately embrace reality. By centering herself in the moment and exploring abstraction through unfamiliar methods, she gained control while remaining present, avoiding the escape of overplanning. Practicing various techniques has allowed her to develop my style of abstraction and realism, especially in direct observation. In her final portrait, the transition from bright tones to grayscale reflects a sense of disinterest, while her freely flowing hair symbolizes her willingness to face the world rather than hide behind escapism.
