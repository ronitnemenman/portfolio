Community Outreach

As the co-president of Chamblee High School's National Art Honor Society, Ronit has been able to reach out to and volunteer in her community in many ways.

Free Art Friday

Once a month throughout the school year, she makes pieces to be hidden around her school building as donations to her fellow students

Art Drops

The City of Chamblee hosts a similar event to Free Art Friday once a year, the main difference being it takes place city wide, which Ronit has participated in both years since its inception

Face Painting

Through NAHS, she has also been able to volunteer to spread community through facepainting at back to school festivals, Taste of Chamblee, and most recently Chamblee Homecoming 2025.
There are no photos available since Ronit was unable to get permission from those who she worked with

Project Sunflower

When people in the Chamblee Community are going through hard times, such as sickness or loss, NAHS bands together to make art of their favorite things.

Competitions

She has also worked in many live art competitions such as the Chamblee Chalk Walk (2023, 2024, & 2025), Columbus State University Throw Down (2024), and the Dogwood Festival Throw Down (2025)

Murals

She has also been able to help in the painting of two large scale murals with NAHS that are located in Chamblee.
